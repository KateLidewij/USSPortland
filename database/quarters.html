USS Portland

Quarters

There are four types of Quarters on the USS Portland:

  • 6 VIP Quarters
  • 14 Senior Officers Quarters
  • 74 Junior Officers Quarters
  • 162 Enlisted Crew Quarters

For room numbers and assigned quarters go here.

VIP Quarters:

The standard version of the VIP Quarters consist of a bathroom with double sink, a toilet and a sonic shower. The living room consists of twin beds, a work desk, a comm station, a circular dining area and a replicator.

The VIP quarters can be adapted to the wishes of the occupant. They can also be remodeled to have two separate bedrooms and a living area to accommodate families.

Senior Officers Quarters:

Senior officersâ€™ quarters are assigned to the command staff and department heads of the USS Portland.

They consist of a living room, bedroom and bathroom. The living room has a circular dining area, a work desk, a comm station and a replicator.

Junior Officers Quarters:

For all the non-senior staff officers of the USS Portland.

Junior officers have solo room with a bed, a desk with chair and a dresser. They do not have access to a replicator. They share a bathroom with another officer.

Enlisted Crew Quarters:

Quarters for the Enlisted crew of the USS Portland.

Enlisted crew share a cabin with one another, two to a room. The bathroom is shared with another cabin, so four crew members in total share one bathroom.

The cabins consist of two beds, each with a stool and a small storage area. The bathroom has a quadruple sink, a sonic shower, a toilet and two large closets.

Enlisted crewmen with the rank of Chief Petty Officer or higher sometimes get a cabin to themselves, if the total number of crewman on the ship allows for it.
